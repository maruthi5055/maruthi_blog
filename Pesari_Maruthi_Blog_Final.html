About Me

I am a researcher and farmer with a deep-rooted passion for microbiology and sustainable agriculture. My journey combines hands-on farming experience with academic research to explore microbial and biotechnological solutions that can enhance crop health, productivity, and long-term sustainability.

Currently, I am pursuing my Ph.D. in Microbiology at Kakatiya University, under the guidance of Dr. Venkataiah Peddaboina. My research focuses on plant-microbe interactions, genome editing in crops, plant growth-promoting rhizobacteria (PGPR), and the use of biocontrol agents such as Trichoderma and Pseudomonas to develop sustainable solutions for crop protection.

My main objective is to develop farmer-friendly and eco-efficient technologies that use beneficial microbes and modern genetic tools like CRISPR-Cas9 to improve crop resistance, reduce chemical input, and boost yields. I strongly believe PGPR and biocontrol agents are vital in creating a sustainable and resilient agricultural future, benefiting both farmers and consumers.
