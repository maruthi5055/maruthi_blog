About Me

[cite_start]

I am a researcher and farmer with a deep-rooted passion for microbiology and sustainable agriculture[cite: 2]. [cite_start]My journey combines hands-on farming experience with academic research to explore microbial and biotechnological solutions that can enhance crop health, productivity, and long-term sustainability[cite: 3].

Currently, I am pursuing my Ph.D. in Microbiology at Kakatiya University, under the guidance of Dr. [cite_start]Venkataiah Peddaboina[cite: 4]. [cite_start]My research focuses on plant-microbe interactions, genome editing in crops, **plant growth-promoting rhizobacteria (PGPR)**, and the use of **biocontrol agents** such as *Trichoderma* and *Pseudomonas* to develop sustainable solutions for crop protection[cite: 5].

[cite_start]

My main objective is to develop **farmer-friendly and eco-efficient technologies** that use beneficial microbes and modern genetic tools like **CRISPR-Cas9** to improve crop resistance, reduce chemical input, and boost yields[cite: 6]. [cite_start]I strongly believe PGPR and biocontrol agents are vital in creating a **sustainable and resilient agricultural future**, benefiting both farmers and consumers[cite: 7].

[cite_start]

As a farmer myself, I’m deeply motivated to address real-world agricultural challenges[cite: 8]. [cite_start]My goal is to **bridge the gap between lab research and field application**, and eventually establish a **biotech startup focused on microbial solutions and genome-edited crops** that directly support the farming community[cite: 9].

Ongoing Research & Projects

Integrating microbiology, plant biotechnology, and practical cultivation methods

Home Research Protocols Publications Contact
[cite_start]

My research integrates microbiology, plant biotechnology, and practical cultivation methods to develop eco-friendly and sustainable crop improvement strategies that benefit both farmers and the environment[cite: 11].

1. Farm Mechanization & Practical Field Solutions

[cite_start]

Development of **generalized, low-cost mechanization tools and techniques** aimed at improving traditional farming systems, increasing operational efficiency, and reducing manual labor across various crops[cite: 12, 13].

2. Disease Management in Rice and Solanaceae Crops

[cite_start]

Research on controlling major diseases in **rice and Solanaceae crops** (such as tomato, brinjal, and chili) using **biocontrol agents** and novel formulation techniques[cite: 14, 15].

    [cite_start]
  • Targeted pathogens include sheath blight, blast, bacterial leaf blight, false smut, wilt, and leaf spot diseases[cite: 16].
    • [cite_start]
  • Use of native strains of *Trichoderma*, *Pseudomonas fluorescens*, and *Bacillus* species[cite: 17].
    • [cite_start]
  • Development of **biopolymer-based encapsulated bioformulations** for increased stability and delivery[cite: 18].
    • [cite_start]
  • Study of **protein-pathogen interactions** to understand suppression mechanisms[cite: 19]. [cite_start]These strategies are a future-ready approach to sustainable disease control in both field and horticultural crops[cite: 19].

3. PGPR-Based Crop Enhancement & Product Development

[cite_start]

Exploration of **Plant Growth-Promoting Rhizobacteria (PGPR)** for improving plant performance[cite: 20, 21].

    [cite_start]
  • Nutrient solubilization, abiotic stress tolerance, and enhanced plant growth[cite: 22].
    • [cite_start]
  • Integration of **secondary metabolites** and **bioagents** in formulation design[cite: 23].
    • [cite_start]
  • Development of **next-generation PGPR-based products** combining beneficial microbes, bioformulations, and microbial metabolites[cite: 24]. [cite_start]These technologies aim to replace or reduce chemical inputs while supporting long-term soil health and crop yield[cite: 24].

4. CRISPR-Cas9 Genome Editing for Crop Improvement

[cite_start]

Use of **CRISPR-Cas9 gene editing** in crops like **tomato and other Solanaceae family members** to improve yield potential, resistance to fungal and bacterial pathogens, and stress resilience (e.g., drought, heat)[cite: 25, 26, 27, 28, 29]. [cite_start]This work focuses on developing **genetically improved varieties** that are farmer-friendly, resource-efficient, and aligned with modern agricultural demands[cite: 29].

5. New Variety Development

[cite_start]

Ongoing efforts to develop **new crop varieties** with improved genetics for better adaptability, resilience, and productivity[cite: 30, 31].

    [cite_start]
  • Selection of high-performing lines through microbial and genomic support[cite: 32].
    • [cite_start]
  • Combining traditional breeding with modern tools like PGPR and gene editing[cite: 33]. [cite_start]The goal is to offer **stable, high-quality varieties** suited for both open-field and protected cultivation systems[cite: 33].

6. Hydroponic Cultivation for Leafy Vegetables

[cite_start]

Design and setup of **low-cost hydroponic systems** for cultivating leafy greens such as spinach, lettuce, and amaranthus in clean, soil-free environments[cite: 34, 35]. [cite_start]Work focuses on nutrient optimization using microbial or organic inputs [cite: 36][cite_start], promoting sustainable, space-efficient food production for urban and peri-urban areas [cite: 37][cite_start], and supporting fresh, chemical-free vegetable supply chains[cite: 38].
