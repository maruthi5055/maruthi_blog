This section highlights my hands-on experience in microbial biotechnology, industrial bioformulation, crop trials, and scientific project handling. My work integrates lab-scale precision with field-level application, supporting sustainable crop development and innovation.

1. Microbial Research & In-House Protocols

  • Isolation, identification, and preservation of plant-beneficial microbes.
  • Culture bank maintenance for long-term microbial storage.
  • Routine media preparation and sterilization for bacterial and fungal cultures.
  • Development of Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) for microbial workflows.
  • Design and implementation of custom protocols for small-scale and field-based experiments.
  • Monitoring and documentation of in-house process workflows.

2. Bioformulation Development & Quality Control

  • Formulation of talc-based, liquid, and encapsulated bioagents.
  • Protocol design for **scaling production**—both small-scale R&D and large-scale manufacturing.
  • Raw material processing and in-process quality checks.
  • Log sheet preparation and internal monitoring at every production stage.
  • Final product evaluation for viability, formulation quality, and COA (Certificate of Analysis).
  • Packaging design, labeling, and storage condition optimization.

3. Field Trials, Crop Evaluation & Variety Development

  • Execution of field and greenhouse trials for biocontrol and PGPR performance.
  • Management of experimental crops in glasshouse and open-field setups.
  • Screening of varieties and germplasm under stress and disease pressure.
  • Maintenance of breeding lines and support for **new variety development**.
  • Coordination of **bulk trials and multilocational evaluations**.

4. PGPR & Biocontrol Agent Evaluation

  • Screening of PGPR traits: nutrient solubilization, IAA, siderophore, and enzyme production.
  • Antagonistic activity assays (e.g., dual culture, pot assays).
  • Compatibility studies and microbial consortia testing.
  • Validation of efficacy under lab, greenhouse, and field conditions.

5. Scientific Writing, Project Design & Data Handling

  • Designing and executing novel experiments based on real-world field needs.
  • Recording, analyzing, and interpreting experimental data.
  • Writing of research publications, review papers, and scientific reports.
  • Preparation of proposals, abstracts, and documentation for grants and presentations.
  • Organizing trial data, results, and statistical summaries for publication and internal use.
